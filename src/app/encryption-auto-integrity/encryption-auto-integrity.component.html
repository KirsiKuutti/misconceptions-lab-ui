Encryption automatically provides integrity and authenticity
People who hold this misconception believe that encryption automatically provides integrity or authenticity along with confidentiality, while this is not always the case. Symmetric encryption algorithms used for confidentiality, such as AES, do provide integrity or authenticity in their most basic modes of operation. Authenticity is not provided because anyone with the AES key (which must be shared by its very nature) can send an encrypted message, and you cannot verify who sent it.
